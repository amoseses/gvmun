GVMUNC X - Conference Details

Debates. Ideas. Solutions.

Greetings international delegates,

Welcome to the tenth Global Virtual Model United Nations Conference (GVMUNC), hosted on October 19th! Join us for the day as we introduce ideas, debate solutions, and finally implement resolutions. Participate in a growing international committee of diplomacy and discussion, and prove your skills as a Model UN delegate.

Committees Available

For this iteration, GVMUN is offering delegates the option between two different committees, ranging from beginner to advanced. They are as follows:

United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund (UNICEF)

Beginner

Delegates in this committee will represent all UN member states, and will address the global rise of malnutrition among children, or combat child labor in the industrial sector.

Futuristic Crisis: Water Wars

Advanced

This committee is set in the near future, 2040, when chronic water shortages begin to cause widespread conflict between nations in a fight for survival. Delegates will be the leaders of all current UN member states.

Awards

Each committee will have three awards, as follows:

  • Best Delegate - 1
  • Outstanding Delegate - 1
  • Best Research and Preparation - 1

Note that a position paper, while not required, is strongly suggested for award eligibility. Delegates must research their topic thoroughly, starting with the provided topic briefs, and prepare for the committee. Additionally, make sure to check the code of conduct, and reach out to us if you have any questions. The email is: gvmunsecretariat@gmail.com

Additionally, please follow our Instagram @gvmun_official to support us and receive updates.

We hope to see you on October 19th for a day of collaboration, debate, and solutions.

Your Secretary-Generals,

Kush Dave and Ayushmaan Mukherjee

GVMUNEC V 2025 - Essay Competition

Debates. Ideas. Solutions.

GVMUN is pleased to announce the fifth iteration of the Global Virtual Model United Nations Essay Competition (GVMUNEC), a contest for aspiring writers and thinkers to share their insight on the world. The competition will be composed of essay submissions from an informed international audience looking to spark change in the world.

Choose One Prompt:

Prompt 1: Global Youth Assemblies

What role should global youth assemblies play in decision-making on issues like AI, climate, and bioethics?

Prompt 2: Cultural Artifacts and Reparations

To what extent can museums and institutions be held responsible for returning looted cultural artifacts, and how should reparations be negotiated?

Requirements:

  • 600-800 word essay answering one prompt
  • Times New Roman, size 12 font
  • Submit as PDF with format: [Name] - [Topic Number] - [Word Count]
  • Works cited page recommended (mandatory if citations are used)

Awards & Recognition:

  • Top 3 essays from each category receive awards
  • Recognition on social media platforms
  • Opportunity to become GVMUN Interns
  • Work directly with our team

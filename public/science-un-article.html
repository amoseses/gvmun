Science and weather forecasting gain defense from the UN chief amid global climate-policy tensions

UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres defends climate science and early-warning systems against political pressures and funding cuts

Shravya Nadagowda

Journalism Intern

December 27, 2025
10 min read

UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres addresses climate science and weather forecasting at WMO Headquarters

The United Nations Secretary-General, Antonio Guterres, discussed the subject of weather-forecasting systems and climate science in Geneva, on October 22nd, recalling the topic of scientific integrity, transforming lives, due to political acts on meteorological agencies and limited proactiveness. Guterres spoke on the long-term crisis of truth, focusing on funding cuts, political debate, and inadequate awareness. These concerns lead towards the COP30 summit in Brazil, where nations will advocate for equal, urgent emission cuts on scientific grounds and resolutions.

Guterres's Call for Scientific Integrity

Guterres emphasized the need for long-term monitoring and security to prevent further weather hazards and investment risks. Due to increased weather changes, health challenges are consistent in occurrence. This news brief highlights the background regarding Guterres's statement, explores diplomatic perspectives, and studies short and long-term results concerning international climate decisions.

WMO Greenhouse Gas Bulletin Findings

At the World Meteorological Organization's (WMO) Headquarters in Geneva, Guterres appreciated the updates respecting updating humanity on early-warning systems for weather conflicts. He conveyed the need to communicate data, to prevent running into wildfires, flood frequency, and environmental heat waves. By analyzing the WMO's latest Greenhouse Gas Bulletin, the chart revealed carbon dioxide levels were at their largest annual rise, surpassing approximately 423.9 parts per million (ppm) in 2024. In the first publication of 2004, the bulletin increased in growth by 377.1 ppm. As an average annual rate calculation, the results from 2011 to 2020 accelerated from 0.8 ppm to 2.4 ppm per year. Roughly half of the CO₂ consumed is embedded in the atmosphere, while the rest is taken up by oceans and land, concerning accelerated global warming due to fewer positive effects.

Climate Alert: Carbon dioxide levels have reached their highest annual increase on record, with atmospheric CO₂ concentrations exceeding 423.9 ppm in 2024 - a dramatic acceleration from historical trends that threatens to intensify global warming impacts.

The Cost of Climate-Driven Disasters

Through these findings, Guterres correlated this information with the cost of climate-driven disasters to humanity. Through timelines, climate-related hazards, including weather, water, and agriculture, have impacted over 2 million lives, with statistics on 90 percent of deaths arising in developing nations. By mobilizing funding for early-warning systems, farmers can produce healthier crops and livestock, and prepare for safe evacuations. When Guterres launched this initiative in 2022, over 60% of countries implemented multi-hazard Early Warning Systems, preparing countries to attain this platform by 2027. However, these investments could lead to further consequences or weaken development. Guterres urged countries to meet at the U.N. Climate Conference, where they will discuss a strategy in seeking $3 trillion annually by 2035 for climate finance projects in developing countries.

Anti-Science Policies and the MAHA Movement

Secretary-General Guterres expressed worries about the push encouraging anti-science policies, promoting the MAHA movement (Make America Healthy Again). This cause encourages wellness and supplement manufacturing companies and corporations to support policies benefiting their profit, finances, and economic consistency. These policies align with standard principles of "health freedom and flexibility." As stated by a thorough Associated Press Investigation, the MAHA movement was addressing legislative bills of the state, engaging with fluoride, vaccines, and raw-milk regulations, contributing in regards to the critiques of "anti-science" topics. MAHA opposers have challenged regulations and public health integrity, while supporters counterclaim, portraying certainty on MAHA empowering small businesses, consumers, and materialistic influence.

The Trump administration executed significant reductions at the National Weather Service, dismissing hundreds of staff members, weather forecasters, and researchers at the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.

Impact on Science: The dismissal of hundreds of weather forecasters and researchers at NOAA represents a significant threat to climate monitoring capabilities and the ability to provide accurate weather forecasts that save lives and protect property.

Economic Impact of Climate Disasters

According to the UNDRR, the cost of disasters averaged $70-80 billion. In this century, the costs have more than doubled to $180-200 billion annually. The International Organization for Migration (IOM) affirmed close to 46 million people were relocated by challenges in 2024, one of the highest results logged. Solutions for disaster risk reduction remain largely underfunded. This reaches an estimated $2 trillion yearly, from social, economic, and deteriorating ecosystems. Natural hazards affect regions differently, heavily affecting locations of conflict, poverty, or food insecurity. Through initiatives of community resilience programs and protective infrastructure, harm is reduced, but global preparation and investment are insufficient moving forward.

Voices from the International Community

Amy Pope, chief of the International Organization of Migration (IOM), quoted: "Wildfires in Europe and the Americas, and devastating earthquakes in Myanmar and Afghanistan, prove that no country is immune, but the heaviest toll falls on communities already struggling with conflict, poverty, and hunger." She voices the decaying lands due to the catastrophes of the world. She believes that decisions for infrastructure, investments, and protection will raise exposure to disasters, claiming: "The impact of disasters depends in large part on the choices we make, how strong our infrastructure is, how much we invest in prevention, and how well we protect the most vulnerable."

Representative Grace Meng, U.S. Representative for New York's 6th Congressional District, released a statement, declaring: "Today, hundreds of employees at the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), including weather forecasters at the National Weather Service (NWS), were given termination notices for no good reason. This is unconscionable." This report symbolizes Grace Meng's agreement with the Secretary-General's statement on the weather advisory. Additionally, Meng added: "These are dedicated, hardworking Americans whose efforts help save lives and property from the devastating impacts of natural disasters across the country. This action will only endanger American lives going forward," voicing her opinion of Guterres' support.

Contrasting Perspectives

The Trump administration disagrees with these opinions, leading an anti-science push. Trump has called climate science a "con job." Unfortunately, due to MAHA (Make America Healthy Again), lives were lost from the vaccination approach. Florida Surgeon General Dr. Joseph Ladapo wrote on social media: "Floridians have the freedom to make informed health choices," supporting human decisions to digest raw milk for potential benefits. Perspective contributes to determining choices.

Short and Long-Term Implications

This crisis is critical to address through mobilizing funding, investments, and agreement on a strategy. In the short term, investing in early-warning systems could strengthen evacuation procedures, protection, and reduce environmental hazards through land, agricultural, and consumer trends that impact health integrity. Long-term outcomes could lead to maintaining global trust in climate science and building international cooperation and alliances; however, political debate might hinder progress. If ignored, many climate risks can endanger health and population without proposal.

Conclusion

Through engagement on climate topics, science, and forecasting, the world's resilience towards science can be reinforced. It's crucial for communication and updates, and the weather forecasting opens many conversations. Moving forward, with political agreement and involvement, there could be tangible change in precaution and anticipation. Global cooperation could move the pathway.

Looking Ahead: The future of climate action depends on maintaining scientific integrity, adequate funding for weather forecasting systems, and international cooperation. The decisions made today will determine humanity's ability to adapt to and mitigate climate change in the decades to come.

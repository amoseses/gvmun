Tariffs on India Jump to 50% Amid Russian Oil Dispute

Shravya Nadagowda

Journalism Intern

August 20, 2025
7 min read

Trade tensions escalate as Trump administration targets Indian oil imports from Russia. (File photo)

In recent times, the Trump campaign has proposed sharp escalations, doubling tariff rates from 25% to 50% on Indian goods, penalizing India's importing of discounted, crude Russian Oil. The Trump administration has expressed frustration through non-alignment in the dispute between India and Russia. This marks a major shift in Donald Trump's 2025 trade strategy.

India's Russian Oil Purchases

Since 2022, India has been purchasing many Russian oil products that were supplanted by Western sanctions when Moscow invaded Ukraine during this time period. Western sanctions created an opportunity for Indian refiners to process discounted crude. These purchases evoked criticism and disapproval from the government under the U.S. President, Donald Trump, in August of 2025. This resulted in a proposed 50% hike in tariffs on Indian imports, affecting both traded goods and budgets.

Global Oil Market Dynamics

U.S. Officials claim Russia profits from discounted Russian Oil. This is because the European Union and the United States continue to purchase Russian goods, valued at billions of dollars. Russia plans to raise exports by 150,000–300,000 barrels a day, aided by improved refinery operations that have infrequent outages. Due to conflicts between Ukraine and Russia, Ukraine has battled and consumed 17% of its refining capacity.

So far, the first twenty days of August, India has assisted in taking 1.73 million barrels per day, unchanged from July. Largely, India has purchased over 85% of its overall needed oil requirement. This represents a significant portion of the global supply, indicating that India is one of the largest buyers. As there is no clear policy in place for economic trading and shifts, Russian crude Oil will be a crucial component of the supply mix. For Russian oil revenues, the Price Cap has increased towards $47.60 per oil barrel due to the market economy and industry. Western services are now restricted to cargoes that are sold above the cap price stated.

Diplomatic Implications

India's oil purchases strained the relationship between the United States and India's India. Indian oil purchases also led to India having closer relationships with U.S. rivals. Pivotal subjects are presented, such as economic industry shifts, budget imbalances, oil price increases, and disruption of the workforce trading system. A concrete solution is needed, and the resolution should be predicted for tangible change. These countries are working towards resolving international affairs, as well as Brazil and East Asian countries, which have been unwillingly involved in these economic constraints and transitions. While no official compromise has been proposed by the United Nations, countries surrounding the economic concerns for India's trading status have raised concerns and opinions in diplomatic presentations and settings.

International Perspectives

Many international perspectives had words on this issue, such as financial analysts from BNB Paribas, perceiving the tariffs as future conflicts, quoted: "The tariffs are part of a broader trade discussion between India and the U.S., and given India's increasing domestic refinery runs amid discounted Russian barrels, we don't see India scuppering its Russian imports in meaningful volumes."

To continue, Prime Minister Narendra Modi spoke on enforcing diplomatic ties with other countries, specifically with Russian President Vladimir Putin. This is due to New Delhi's plan of relying on conversation to resolve additional amounts of Trump tariffs.

European Union Sanctions

The European Union confirmed on July 18th that it had adopted its 18th package of economic sanctions against Russia. Nayara Energy, based in Vadinar, Gujarat, said sanctions were imposing restrictive measures on its workforce. This company, along with Reliance Industries Limited, is one of the two largest private oil refiners in India. Many broader consequences could occur for these companies due to the Indian Energy Sanctions, cutting off the European market and logistics support. The European Union explained that full-fledged sanctions target Russian and international companies managing traders of Russian Crude Oil, shadow fleet vessels, and customer logistics.

Indian Government Response

As honesty and transparency play a critical role in country trust, External Affairs Ministry Spokesperson Randhir Jaiswal stated, "India does not subscribe to any unilateral sanction measures. We are a responsible actor and remain fully committed to our legal obligations." Jaiswal emphasizes the importance of energy security and follows legal protocols.

Additionally, Minister S Jaishankar conveyed: "Nations buy refined petroleum products from India of their own free will. Nobody forces you to buy it. Europe buys, America buys, so you don't like it, don't buy it." This possibly contradicts Jaiswal's declaration. Budget and economic issues lead to struggles in elections and campaigns, as well as creating balancing acts with countries through open forum sessions. The Modi government explains that India is a "critical partner" in balancing China and New Delhi through strategic proportions.

Global Market Implications

Oil refinery companies in India have been both a huge target and a necessity. Jennifer Nicks, a columnist and political commentator, spoke: "While Western governments attempt to isolate Russia, the flows of crude and refined products continue to blur political boundaries."

This issue matters globally, as India is one of the top global oil importers, which also affects the sustainability of the energy market. As India continues to depend on Russian discounted oil, the actions directly impact oil prices globally and demand. If India continues to purchase large quantities of cheap oil, this could weaken the impacts of Western sanctions' sanctions and strengthen Russian oil revenue flow.

Economic and Geopolitical Risks

Conflicts may arise between price shocks, energy security, and global supply chains, with short-term effects that could lengthen over time. To elaborate, achieving a 50% mark of a tariff price could weaken the market economy, trade measures, and company finances. Fewer investors and partnerships will pitch towards companies if the Return on Investment for oil pricing is unpredictable, which is short-term unless a consistent market strategy is not formally executed.

In addition, eroding Western sanctions on Russia, efforts to bypass Western sanctions, and creating a lack of trust towards countries for illogical oil purchases. If trust isn't further ensured, governments may consider risky solutions for energy and oil diversification, potentially forming alternative trading routes that reduce Western influence.

Long-term Consequences

If economies continue to bypass Western sanctions and their Oil, the global community could lose a long-term tool needed from the United States, which is non-military pressure and reassurance of alliance. Alluding to bonds and relations, a switch of geopolitical alliances could cause deeper struggles in International Affairs and communication. This is a short-term solution, unless India chooses to deepen its energy and trade ties with non-Western partners. Adding on, the United States could weaken the counterbalancing relationship with China, a country that is part of the Indo-Pacific territory. Lastly, all these concerns combined could raise legal enforcement and consequences if oil purchases don't comply with the law.

If left unresolved, trading tensions could result in impaired global energy markets, diplomatic conflicts, and weakened international sanctions. By addressing this issue through advocacy, diplomatic leaders, government, and politicians, we can address the root cause of this global altercation and preserve the geographical alliances initiated through past and present years.

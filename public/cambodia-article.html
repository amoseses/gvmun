Tens of Thousands Flee Homes as Cambodia–Thailand Clashes Escalate

Alisa Pomelnikova

Summer 2025 Journalism Intern

August 15, 2025
12 min read

Tensions escalate at the Cambodia-Thailand border amid territorial disputes. (File photo)

There was an armed conflict on the border of Cambodia and Thailand in 2025 caused by a long-term dispute about the border territories. Cambodia and Thailand accused each other in violation of agreements and provocations. As a result of the conflict, there were many victims. But what really happened?

The armed clashes occurred on the night of July 24 near the ancient temple Ta Muen Thom. It is located on the territory of Thailand, next to the territory contested by Cambodia. According to the Thai authorities, 12 citizens of the country were killed as a result: 11 civilians (including a teenager and eight year old child) and one military. In the video that appeared on the Internet there were explosions and shootouts. The authorities of Thailand decided to evacuate the citizens of four provinces on the border with Cambodia: Surin, Buriram, Sisaket and Ubon Ratchathani. Thailand later reported almost 190 thousand evacuated residents from the border provinces.

The parties accused each other of escalating the conflict. The Thai Army stated that it was Cambodian troops that opened fire near the temple Ta Muen Thom. However, the Cambodia Ministry of Defense claimed that the soldiers acted for self-defense and opened fire in response to shelling from Thailand. Cambodia also accused Thailand of using heavy weapons and applying an airstrike on its territory. The country's authorities called the Thai actions "cruel and illegal aggression" and "egregious violation of the UN Charter and the fundamental principles of international law." At the same time, Bangkok accused the Cambodia Army of shelling civil facilities, including hospitals. On Thursday morning, Thailand announced the closure of the border with Cambodia. The country's embassy in Phnom Penh demanded their citizens to leave Cambodia, and those who intended to visit her to transfer their trips.

Brief History

Bangkok and Phnom Penh have been disposing of the affiliation of the territories of the Prea Vihear and Ta Muen Thom temples for more than a hundred years. The total length of the dispute border is more than 800 kilometers, the region is sparsely populated, in a large part it is inaccessible and insufficiently cartographic. Many elements of the border have remained since the days of French colonial rule - from 1863 to 1953, Cambodia was a protectorate of France, while Thailand - at that time the Kingdom of Siam - formally remained independent. Over the 90 years, several mutual concessions of territories occurred, and as a result of which the actual border was changed several times. In addition, the border established by France in 1907 was later challenged by Thailand.

In 1959, the situation was submitted to the UN International Court of Justice (ICJ) in The Hague, which three years later (1962) recognized the right of Cambodia to the temples' complex. When UNESCO in 2008 wanted to declare it as an object of world cultural heritage, the conflict broke out again and many people died during the shootout on the border. Cambodia again turned to the ICJ, who again decided in favor of Cambodia. However, clashes on the border near the disputed areas did not stop.

Cause of the July 2025 Conflict

Tensions between the two countries have been rising in recent months. Especially after a Cambodian soldier was killed in a clash in late May when Cambodian soldiers tried to dig trenches on a disputed section of the border. The Thai government stated that the soldier had stepped on a mine. As a result, both countries sent additional troops to the border region. In addition, Bangkok recalled its ambassador from Phnom Penh and expelled the Cambodian ambassador from the country.

As the conflict escalated, Cambodia banned the import of fruits and vegetables from Thailand and banned the showing of Thai films. The work of many border checkpoints on the two countries' border was suspended or restricted. Just a day before the conflict escalated, several Thai soldiers were injured by mines. Thailand accused Cambodia of having deliberately planted the mines.

But the Thai public is wondering whether the border dispute is a pretext for secret deals between two countries in a disputed area where rich fossil fuel reserves are believed to be located. Critics say the government's inaction is only fueling public suspicions about the hidden interests of the Shinawatra family (Thailand) and the Khun family (Cambodia).

On July 26, Thai Royal Army General Vinthai Suvari announced that Cambodian shells had fallen on the territory of neighboring Laos. The Thai Armed Forces called the incident "warfare outside the rules" and called for its immediate cessation. Bangkok also claimed that Phnom Penh had deliberately organized the shelling of Laotian territory in order to discredit the Thai military. The Chinese agency Xinhua, citing a source in the Laotian army, reported that the latest disseminated information about a shootout between Laotian and Cambodian soldiers was not true. However, the agency cited data that as part of the clashes between Thailand and Cambodia from July 24 to 25, 10 artillery shells exploded on Laotian territory - whose, is not specified. Cambodia, in turn, denied the reports of its shells falling in Laos, noting that Laos had not made any accusations.

US President Trump's Role in Negotiation

On July 26, US President Donald Trump said that Thailand and Cambodia had agreed to meet "immediately" to work out a ceasefire, only after which Washington would resume trade negotiations with the two countries. "Both sides want an immediate ceasefire and peace. They also want to return to trade negotiations with the US, which we believe is impossible until the arm conflict stops," Trump wrote on Saturday on his "TRUTH Social" social media account.

The ceasefire between Thailand and Cambodia came into effect at midnight on July 29 after five days of clashes. The parties agreed on the ceasefire with the mediation of Malaysian Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim (ASEAN Chairman), who provided the parties with a platform for negotiations. As a result, on July 28, Acting Prime Minister of Thailand Phumtham Wechayachai and Prime Minister of Cambodia Khun Manet held a meeting in Kuala Lumpur, where a new ceasefire agreement was reached.

After the parties announced their agreement to cease fire, US President Donald Trump announced the settlement of the conflict and called it his merit: "I am pleased to announce that after the intervention of US President Donald Trump, the countries have agreed to a ceasefire and peace," the American leader wrote on his social network "TRUTH Social". Soon after, Cambodia formally announced its intention to nominate US President Donald Trump for the Nobel Peace Prize for his key role in ending a recent border conflict with Thailand. According to the country's Deputy Prime Minister and chief trade negotiator, Sun Chanthol, Trump "deserves" to be nominated for one of the most prestigious international awards for promoting peace between countries.

As Alexander Korolev, Director of the Center for Comprehensive European and International Studies at the National Research HSE, told "the catalyst for the peace talks between Thailand and Cambodia was indeed blackmailed by US President Donald Trump: both countries are interested in achieving the softest possible trade conditions, since the United States is their largest export market". According to him, "there is no point in talking about a long-term, and even less so, a final resolution to the conflict: this is only a ceasefire, and in the very near future we may see both low-intensity tension and an exchange of diplomatic barbs from both sides, as well as a resumption of hostilities." Indeed, on July 29 and 30, Thailand accused Cambodia of violating the ceasefire, and Cambodia, in turn, accused the Thai side of capturing Cambodian soldiers.

Importance of US Trade to Cambodia and Thailand

The US is the largest market for both countries. According to the WTO, in 2024, the US accounted for 37.2% of Cambodia's exports ($9.9 billion) and 18.3% of Thailand's exports ($54.8 billion). At the same time, the US has a significant trade deficit with both countries: according to statistics from the US Trade Representative, in 2024 it was $12.3 billion with Cambodia and $45.6 billion with Thailand. According to Trump's executive order of April 2, the US will introduce individual duties of 49% on imports from Cambodia and 36% from Thailand.

Current Safety Situation

One of the Russian tourists, who was in Bangkok when the conflict began and returned to Russia on the day of the armistice, told that the border conflict had no effect on his vacation: "The border area where the fighting is taking place is only a small part of the country, the conflict has no effect on the rest of the country." According to him, no one is stopping those wishing to get from Thailand to Cambodia by using direct flights from Bangkok.

Air traffic between the warring kingdoms has not been interrupted: despite the closure of the northwestern part of Cambodia's airspace since July 25, 2025, regular flights between the capitals are operating without hindrance. No one is preventing travel across the Cambodian border with neighboring Vietnam or Laos. Direct flights to Cambodia are also operated by China, India, South Korea and the UAE.

Additionally, according to the Russian diplomat in Cambodia Igor Karavaev, "the situation in the capital of Cambodia is calm, government agencies, departments, educational and medical institutions, shops are functioning as usual. We are not seeing any signs of any escalation. No interruptions in communications, water or electricity supply have been recorded. The embassy is operating as usual."

Most Recent Negotiations

Malaysia has hosted negotiations between Thailand and Cambodia in Kuala Lumpur from August 4 to 7, seeking to solidify the fragile ceasefire. At the meeting, Cambodian Defence Minister Tea Seiha and Thailand's acting defence minister Nattaphon Narkphanit agreed to allow ASEAN-led observer teams, coordinated by Malaysia, to monitor disputed border areas and ensure hostilities do not resume through talks of a permanent cessation. The talks, attended by observers from China and the United States, produced commitments to improve military communication, hold follow-up meetings within two weeks, and work toward a permanent end to clashes that last month killed at least 43 people and displaced over 300,000 on both sides of the frontier.

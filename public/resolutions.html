What Are Resolutions?

Resolutions are formal documents that outline comprehensive solutions to global challenges discussed in Model UN committees. They represent the collaborative effort of delegates working together to address pressing international issues through diplomatic consensus and innovative policy proposals.

Each resolution contains operative clauses that detail specific actions, recommendations, and frameworks for addressing the topic at hand. Our collection showcases the exceptional work produced by GVMUN delegates across various committees and conferences.

International Labor Organization (ILO)
Rights of Migrant Workers
GVMUNC II — 2024

Sponsors: Republic of Hungary, State of Israel, Swiss Confederation, United States of America, Republic of Austria, State of Qatar
Signatories: Kingdom of Denmark, Argentine Republic, People’s Republic of Bangladesh, Republic of Poland, Ukraine, Republic of the Philippines

This resolution addresses the rights and protections of migrant workers, calling for accessible legal aid, research, education efforts, and stronger legal frameworks. It urges nations to enhance labor law enforcement, provide training for law enforcement, and establish fair pathways to regularization, while promoting transparency, anti-discrimination, and support for migrant families.

Historical Security Council (HSC)
Comprehensive Peace and Reconstruction Plan for Iraq
GVMUNC III — 2024

Sponsors: Brazil, India, Libya
Signatories: Belgium, France, Iraq, Qatar, Austria

This resolution establishes a UN peacekeeping force in Iraq, enacts the Tripoli Accords for peace talks, proposes a ceasefire and withdrawal plan, creates watchdog and monitoring bodies, and ensures protection for refugees and human rights. It also calls for public-private partnerships, collaboration with UNESCO to restore heritage sites, and employment initiatives for Iraqis affected by the war.

European Union (EU)
Political Extremism
GVMUNC I — 2024

Sponsors: Slovakia, Netherlands, Czech Republic, Croatia
Signatories: Ayushmaan Mukherjee, Marko Gvozdenovic

This resolution addresses political extremism in Europe through several key initiatives: establishing educational programs for deradicalization, creating "Project Diversity" to foster tolerance, implementing an anti-corruption framework, enhancing the European Counter Terrorism Centre, reforming legal frameworks for discrimination, combating poverty, and regulating extremist content on social media using AI and human oversight.
